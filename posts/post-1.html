With the game of the century coming to a close about 2 hours ago, I figured I would give a few thoughts on it and break down the roller coaster of a mental state that it took me on. Before I get into it I want to make sure that the person who decided the Gold Medal Game would be scheduled for 8:10 AM EST gets put into prison for the rest of their life. This caused all of us North Americans to start our mornings with a beer rather than a coffee, which in retrospect may not be the worst thing in the world if I'm honest. THE NETMINDER First off, I'll start with the netminder of the winning team, Connor Hellebuyck. This man has cemented himself as America's big game goalie. He stood on his head the entire game, with his only slip up being on a Cale Makar snipe from a dangerous spot on the ice. Canada reigned down hellfire on Helle with 42 shots through 3 periods and a bit of overtime — he stopped 41 of them, earning an unbelievable 0.976 SV%. "IN THOSE 41 SAVES, HE STUFFED SOLO BREAKAWAYS FROM CONNOR MCDAVID AND MACKLIN CELEBRINI — AND MADE ONE OF THE GREATEST SAVES I HAVE EVER SEEN ON A GOLDEN CHANCE FOR DEVON TOEWS." That Toews chance was set up on a silver platter by Mitch Marner with a perfect tape-to-tape pass. Hellebuyck said no. THE GOALS A goalie can only do so much for a team, and when goals needed to be scored, Team USA answered the bell. Early in the first, USA was getting sustained offensive pressure on the Canadians, causing them to dump and change at every opportunity. On one of these dumps, Quinn Hughes got the jump on the puck and swung it wide to Auston Matthews along the boards, who kicked it out to Matt Boldy for a 1-on-2 breakaway. Boldy sauced it to himself and tipped it up out of midair to keep the best defensive pairing in the NHL off the puck — and once he got clear, he deked right to pull Binnington over and slid it just left of him. United States, 1–0. After a bit of a lull, the next goal came from Cale Makar late in the second period, assisted by his partner Devon Toews. After a battle down near the faceoff circle, the puck popped out to Toews in the corner, who made a simple cross-ice pass to Makar — who proceeded to dive toward the net and bury a disgusting shot under the blocker of Hellebuyck to even the game. Tied 1–1. OVERTIME Following the Makar goal, USA seemed to be playing on their heels with Canada firing on all cylinders. They were being outshot, outplayed — and I was genuinely worried we wouldn't even make it to overtime. By some miracle Team USA held out, and right when I realized we had made it, I also realized we'd be playing 3-on-3 against arguably the scariest Canadian team ever assembled. The overtime intermission felt like a decade. When it finally came around, me and every hockey fan in North America were sitting up in our chairs like we were the last man alive in search and destroy. After a breakaway by McDavid where he nearly sealed the game, Jake Sanderson ran a give-and-go with Zach Werenski and was able to chip it past Makar into the offensive zone. It floated into a dead zone, Werenski fought for it, and fed Jack Hughes for a slot chance. He did not disappoint. "JACK HUGHES SCORED THE GOLDEN GOAL — GIVING THE UNITED STATES THEIR FIRST OLYMPIC HOCKEY GOLD IN 46 YEARS TO THE DAY — NEXT TO HIS BROTHER QUINN, WHO EMBRACED HIM WITH THE REST OF THE TEAM." I should also mention that late in the third period Jack caught a stick to the mouth from Sam Bennett, breaking his front tooth.
